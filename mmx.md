## 0. One-paragraph summary

Monk ModeX is a **personalized, evidence-based monk mode protocol
generator** for Western (US/UK/AU/CA) audiences aged ~18-35. The user
takes a 90-second diagnostic quiz, gets a personalized "Reward System
Diagnosis" + 30-day Foundation Protocol generated by GPT-4o based on
peer-reviewed addiction medicine and behavior change research, and pays
$19 (Foundation, 30-day) or $39 (Operator, full 90-day with Body Reset
+ Mind Reset phases) to unlock the full protocol. The unlocked product
is a **premium, dark, Apple-quality web dashboard** the user lives in
for 30-90 days — daily check-ins, calendar grid, journal, expandable
sections for cuts/foundation/diagnosis. The moat is **honesty grounded
in real research** (Lembke, Volkow, Berridge, Lally, Gollwitzer, Fogg)
in a market saturated with TikTok bro-science. Ships as a Next.js web
app on Vercel; future ports to React Native (Android, iOS) and Tauri
(Mac/Windows/Linux) will reuse the same Supabase backend and ~40-50%
of the codebase.

## 1. Strategic foundation

### 1.1 The positioning

Most monk mode content on the internet is bro-science: "21 days to a
new you," "literal dopamine detox," "NoFap superpowers." Monk ModeX is
the **honest, evidence-based version** for the same audience. The
product cites real research, flags overclaims, and personalizes the
protocol to the individual user's specific reward system dysregulation.

The pitch: *"The peer-reviewed version of monk mode. Built on 40 years
of addiction research. Personalized to you in 90 seconds."*

The moat is **honesty**. Wellness/self-help is saturated with
overconfident claims and cargo-cult protocols. MMX wins by being the
credible alternative — the one product that says "actually, here's
what the data says, here's what's not proven, here's *your* protocol."

### 1.2 Target audience

- Geography: US > UK > Australia > Canada > Western Europe. **Do NOT
  target India.** Indians don't pay enough; conversion math doesn't
  work. Lemon Squeezy auto-localizes pricing for other regions.
- Demographics: men 18-35, secondary women 20-30. Self-improvement
  / monk mode / looksmaxxing / NoFap / Hamza / Huberman-adjacent
  audience.
- Psychographic: dopamine-fried, ambitious, frustrated with bro-science
  but still in the discipline/grindset cultural orbit. Will pay for a
  product that respects their intelligence.

### 1.3 Pricing

- **$19** — Foundation tier. Phase 1 (Diagnosis) + Phase 2 (Foundation)
  only. 30-day protocol.
- **$39** — Operator tier. Full 90-day program: Phase 1 + 2 + 3 (Body
  Reset) + 4 (Mind Reset). Accessible as upgrade after $19 purchase OR
  as direct purchase on the preview page.
- 30-day money-back guarantee on both. Reduces refund anxiety, signals
  confidence.
- Prices in USD. Lemon Squeezy auto-localizes for UK/EU/AU.
- **No subscription** ever. One-time purchase. Match the audience's
  expectations and avoid the "another monthly subscription" objection.

### 1.4 Revenue targets

- Short-term goal: ₹50,000/month (~$600/month) net.
- Math: at 92% net after Lemon Squeezy + Wise fees, $600 net = ~$650
  gross = ~34 sales/month at $19, OR a mix of ~25 × $19 + ~5 × $39.
- Achievable with ~5,000 monthly visitors at 0.7% conversion. Realistic
  for a focused IG content engine + small paid spend.
- Mid-term goal: $3-5K MRR. Trigger to start v2 (native apps).

### 1.5 What this product is NOT

- Not gamified. No XP, no levels, no streaks-with-resets, no badges,
  no celebration animations, no variable-reward push notifications.
  **The app must not be a supernormal stimulus** — that's the inverse
  of what the product cures.
- Not a habit tracker. It's a personalized protocol delivery + light
  tracking. Habit trackers are commodities; protocols are products.
- Not a mobile app. v1 is web-only, mobile-responsive. Native apps
  come in v2 once revenue justifies.
- Not a social product. No community, no leaderboards, no sharing.
- Not a CMS-driven content site. Content (Bullshit Detector pages)
  ships as MDX in the repo.
- Not Titan. Different audience, different aesthetic, different
  business model. **Do not import Titan code or Titan design language.**

## 2. The research foundation (what drives product decisions)

This is the condensed version of the research substrate. Full research
lives in two compass artifacts; this section captures the
findings that directly shape v1.

### 2.1 The dopamine truth

- Dopamine is **wanting**, not pleasure. Berridge's incentive salience
  theory. Doomscrolling is hijacked motivation, not real enjoyment.
- "Dopamine detox" is colloquial pseudoscience. The real mechanism is
  **reward system recalibration** — abstinence from supernormal
  stimuli (porn, TikTok, slots) lets D2 receptors recover. Volkow's
  PET work confirms.
- Lembke's clinical norm: **30 days minimum**, 90 days for substantial
  healing. Days 1-14 feel worse (allostatic withdrawal), weeks 3-4
  things click.
- Variable-reward schedules (Skinner; Zald 2004) are why TikTok hooks.
  Users aren't weak — they're up against engineered compulsion.

**Product implication:** Frame everything as "recalibration" not
"detox." Default cut window is 30 days, with 60/90 options for deeper
compulsions. Brief users explicitly that days 1-14 will feel worse.

### 2.2 The habit formation truth

- "21 days" is folklore from a 1960 plastic surgeon's anecdote.
- Lally 2010 actual data: **median 66 days**, range 18-254.
- Missing one day does NOT derail habit formation. **High inconsistency
  does.**

**Product implication:** Foundation tier is 30 days (entry point,
matches Lembke), Operator tier is 90 days (matches Lally upper
plateau). Calendar UI must explicitly handle missed days without a
"streak resets to zero" mechanic — that mechanism is the abstinence
violation effect (AVE) and is the single biggest cause of full relapse.

### 2.3 What actually works (effect sizes, ranked)

1. **Implementation intentions** ("If X, then Y") — d=0.65, strongest
   behavior-change tool in psychology (Gollwitzer 2006 meta-analysis).
2. **Identity-based change** ("I am a person who…") — moderate
   evidence, big for long-term maintenance.
3. **Tiny habits anchored to existing routines** (Fogg) — high
   adherence, low willpower required.
4. **Stimulus control / self-binding** (Lembke) — physical /
   chronological / categorical separation from the stimulus.
5. **Goal monitoring with social accountability** — d=0.40 (Harkin
   2016 meta, 138 studies, 19,951 participants).

**Product implication:** Every cut MUST have an if-then plan. Every
non-negotiable MUST be anchored to an existing routine (Fogg's
"After I [anchor], I will [tiny habit]"). Identity statement is
captured in onboarding and persistent in dashboard UI.

### 2.4 The physiological levers (real numbers)

| Lever | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sleep | 7-9 hrs, consistency > duration | Phone out of bedroom non-negotiable. Fixes ~30% of "motivation problems" alone. |
| Morning sunlight | 5-10 min in first 60 min of waking | Free. Sets circadian. |
| Cardio | 150 min/wk moderate | Floor. Pearce 2022. |
| Resistance | 2× full body / wk | Floor. ~860 MET-min/wk optimal. |
| Cold exposure | 1-3 min cold shower, 3-5×/wk | Real benefit is distress tolerance, not the "250%" number. NEVER within 4 hrs of strength training. |
| Meditation | 10 min FA breath, 1-2× daily | MBSR non-inferior to escitalopram for anxiety (JAMA 2022). |
| Reading | 20-30 min/day | Print > digital. Replaces evening scrolling. |
| Deep work | 90 min single-task blocks | Phone in another room. Leroy attention residue. |
| TRE (optional) | 14:10 or 16:8, 4-5 days/wk | Skip if any ED history, low BMI, or quiz-flagged dysregulation. |

**Product implication:** Foundation Protocol prescribes minimum
effective doses, not maximalist heroics. Each prescription ties back
to a research citation in the dashboard's "why" expansion.

### 2.5 What's overhyped (calibrate user expectations)

- "Grit" — largely conscientiousness rebranded
- "Growth mindset" — small effects, conditional
- "NoFap superpowers" — no RCT support; shame framing actively harmful
  (Prause 2024)
- Cold's "250% dopamine" — single 2000 study, 1 hr at 14°C
- "Dopamine detox" — pseudoscience term; mechanism is real, framing isn't

**Product implication:** Build a **Bullshit Detector** page — public,
SEO-indexed, lists overhyped claims with evidence-based rebuttals.
Doubles as content marketing (each entry → reel) and trust builder.

### 2.6 The 6-phase protocol (your full backbone, only 1+2 in v1)

1. **Diagnosis** (Days -7 to 0) — establish honest baseline. **v1.**
2. **Foundation** (Weeks 1-2) — sleep, sunlight, hydration, top 1-3
   cuts, ONE tiny anchor habit. **v1.**
3. **Body Reset** (Weeks 3-6) — exercise, cold, optional TRE.
   **$39 tier in v1, expansion in dashboard.**
4. **Mind Reset** (Weeks 5-8, overlapping) — meditation, journaling,
   reading, first deep work blocks. **$39 tier in v1.**
5. **Output** (Weeks 9-12) — direct rebuilt capacity at chosen project.
   **v2 expansion. Don't build in v1.**
6. **Integration** (Month 3+) — rule-based moderate reintroduction,
   identity solidification. **v2 expansion. Don't build in v1.**

### 2.7 Critical safety / liability gates

The quiz screens for and handles:

- **Severe alcohol/benzo dependence** — these can be medically
  dangerous to withdraw from. If quiz flags this, app shows a referral
  to professional medical help and refuses to proceed with alcohol as
  a cut target.
- **Eating disorder history** — disable TRE recommendation entirely.
- **Severe depression (PHQ-2 high)** — show "we recommend professional
  support before our protocol" message; allow them to continue with
  modified, BA-emphasized protocol but flag the recommendation.
- **Severe anxiety (GAD-2 high)** — same handling.

These gates are non-negotiable. Implement in v1, day one.

### 2.8 The non-negotiable principles (carry through every decision)

1. Sequence matters. Foundation before body before mind before output.
2. Lapse ≠ relapse. Calendar UI never resets to zero.
3. Identity > outcomes. "I am becoming…" framing throughout.
4. Autonomous motivation > controlled (SDT). Never use shame language.
5. Personalization is scientific necessity, not marketing copy.
6. **The product itself must NOT be a supernormal stimulus.**

## 3. Brand & aesthetic system

### 3.1 The aesthetic direction

**"Apple Fitness meets a private members' club."** Premium, restrained,
dark-mode native, with warmth and depth — not cold/sterile, not
loud/gamified.

**Vibe references:** Hermès packaging, the inside of a Bentley, a
Bordeaux wine label, monastery architectural details. Cal Newport's
website. Things 3. Bear notes app. Linear. Stoic journal app.

**Inverse references (do NOT do this):** Habitica, Duolingo, any
gamified habit tracker, hustle-bro Twitter, generic SaaS dashboards.

### 3.4 Spacing & layout

**Spacing scale** (based on a 4px unit):

```
xs: 4px   sm: 8px   md: 16px   lg: 24px
xl: 32px  2xl: 48px  3xl: 64px  4xl: 96px
```

**Apple-generous defaults:**
- Section vertical spacing: 48-64px
- Card padding: 24-32px
- Button padding: 12px vertical / 24px horizontal
- Form field gap: 16-20px

Resist the urge to fill space. Whitespace = premium.

### 3.5 Borders & corners

- Border radius: `8px` cards, `6px` buttons, `4px` inputs.
  Never pill-shaped (too playful), never square (too brutalist).
- Borders: `1px solid var(--border-subtle)` on cards.
  Subtle, not loud.

### 3.6 Shadows

Almost imperceptible in dark mode:

```css
--shadow-sm: 0 1px 2px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.3);
--shadow-md: 0 4px 12px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.4);
--shadow-lg: 0 12px 32px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.5);
```

### 3.7 Animations

- All transitions: 200-300ms ease-out
- Subtle fades only. No bouncing, no scaling, no flourishes.
- No celebration moments. No confetti. No sound.
- Page transitions: 200ms fade.
- Hover: subtle background color shift, 150ms.

### 3.8 Iconography

**Phosphor Icons** (regular weight) or **Lucide**. Outlined, single
weight, never filled. No emoji anywhere in the product.

### 3.9 Imagery

Monochrome only. High-contrast B&W of:
- Architectural details (monastic spaces, brutalist buildings)
- Natural textures (mountains, fog, water)
- NO lifestyle photos of people

Source from Unsplash + heavy desaturation filter applied in CSS.

### 3.10 Voice & copy

**Tone:** Operator-grade. Direct, unsentimental, evidence-grounded.
Never preachy. Never "wellness." Never bro.

**Word choices:**
- "Reward recalibration" not "dopamine detox"
- "High-friction behavior" not "addiction"
- "Lapse" not "relapse"
- "Operator" not "user" (in customer-facing copy)
- "Protocol" not "plan"
- "Cuts" not "things to give up"
- "Foundation" not "basics"

**Never use:** crushing it, leveling up, life hack, game-changer,
literally, journey, transformation, unleash, manifest, vibrate at a
higher frequency. Any wellness-Instagram language.

**Always use:** specific numbers, specific timelines, specific
research citations where natural.

### 4.2 Why these specifically (rationale for each non-obvious pick)

- **Next.js 15 over Vite/Astro/Remix:** Server components for AI calls,
  streaming UI for diagnosis generation, built-in API routes for
  webhooks, SEO for Bullshit Detector pages, biggest LLM training data
  surface for Claude Code to generate fluently.
- **Supabase over Firebase/Convex:** You know it from Titan. Same
  Postgres, same auth model. No relearning cost.
- **Magic-link only over passwords/OAuth:** Premium signal. Removes
  password reset flow. Lower friction. Linear, Substack, Notion (early)
  all do this. Customer email auto-collected at Lemon Squeezy
  checkout — you don't even need a "sign up" form.
- **OpenAI over Claude API for v1:** Structured Outputs (JSON schema
  enforcement) is currently more reliable. Streaming UI works great.
  Swap later if needed; the abstraction is one file.
- **Lemon Squeezy over Stripe direct:** Merchant of record handles
  US/UK/EU tax for you. No GST headache from India. Faster setup.
  ~7% all-in beats Stripe's ~3% only when you do high volume + handle
  tax yourself.
- **Resend over SendGrid:** Modern API, React Email integration, free
  tier covers launch.
- **Plausible over Google Analytics:** No cookie banner needed,
  premium signal, simple dashboard. GA destroys the brand.
- **Sentry from day one:** Catches errors before users complain. Free
  tier is generous. Five-minute setup.

## 5. Code architecture (v1 with v2 portability baked in)

### 5.1 The portability principle

**Structure code so v2 (React Native + Tauri) is a port, not a
rewrite.** Specifically: pure logic and data layers must be platform-
agnostic. Only the component layer is web-specific.

When v2 happens:
- React Native (Expo) gets new components but reuses every
  `lib/`, `services/`, type definition, and Supabase query.
- Tauri can literally point at the deployed Vercel frontend as its
  webview, then add a thin Rust shell for native menus / tray /
  filesystem if needed. Even simpler than the RN port.

### 5.3 The portability rules (commit to these from day 1)

1. **`lib/` has zero imports from `react`, `next`, or any DOM API.**
   Pure TypeScript only. Test that this rule holds with a lint rule.
2. **`services/` has zero imports from `react` or DOM APIs.** It can
   import from `@supabase/supabase-js`, `openai`, `resend`, etc., but
   not from `next/server` (use a thin adapter for that).
3. **All AI prompts live in `lib/ai/prompts/`.** When v2 ships, these
   ports verbatim.
4. **Supabase client factories abstracted.** `services/supabase/client.ts`
   exports a function that returns a configured client. The factory
   takes platform hints (web vs RN) so v2 can pass the right config.
5. **No web-specific globals in lib/services.** No `window`, no
   `document`, no `localStorage`. Use Supabase for state, not browser
   storage.
6. **Tailwind classes throughout.** When v2 ships, NativeWind (Tailwind
   for RN) reuses ~80% of class strings.

### 7.3 Quiz scoring logic (TypeScript, in `lib/quiz/scoring.ts`)

```typescript
import type { QuizResponses } from "./schema";

export interface ScoringOutput {
  primaryCut: string | null;
  secondaryCuts: string[];
  flags: {
    phq2High: boolean;
    gad2High: boolean;
    severeAud: boolean;
    edHistory: boolean;
  };
  substrateScore: {
    sleep: 'critical' | 'weak' | 'okay' | 'strong';
    sunlight: 'critical' | 'weak' | 'okay' | 'strong';
    movement: 'critical' | 'weak' | 'okay' | 'strong';
  };
  recommendedDuration: 30 | 60 | 90;
}

export function scoreQuiz(responses: QuizResponses): ScoringOutput {
  // Primary + secondary cuts from Q5/Q4
  const primaryCut = responses.q5 ?? null;
  const secondaryCuts = (responses.q4 ?? [])
    .filter(c => c !== primaryCut)
    .slice(0, 2);

  // PHQ-2
  const phq2 = (responses.q19a ?? 0) + (responses.q19b ?? 0);
  const phq2High = phq2 >= 3;

  // GAD-2
  const gad2 = (responses.q20a ?? 0) + (responses.q20b ?? 0);
  const gad2High = gad2 >= 3;

  // Severe AUD
  const severeAud = responses.q7 === '15+' && responses.q7_followup === true;

  // ED history
  const edHistory = responses.ed_followup === true;

  // Substrate scoring
  const sleep = scoreSleep(responses.q8, responses.q9, responses.q10);
  const sunlight = scoreSunlight(responses.q11);
  const movement = scoreMovement(responses.q13);

  // Duration recommendation
  const duration = recommendDuration(responses.q6, primaryCut);

  return {
    primaryCut,
    secondaryCuts,
    flags: { phq2High, gad2High, severeAud, edHistory },
    substrateScore: { sleep, sunlight, movement },
    recommendedDuration: duration,
  };
}

function scoreSleep(hours, consistency, phoneInBedroom) {
  if (hours === 'under_5' || hours === '5_6') return 'critical';
  if (consistency === '2_plus_hours') return 'critical';
  if (phoneInBedroom === 'on_nightstand') return 'weak';
  if (hours === '7_8' && consistency === 'under_30_min') return 'strong';
  return 'okay';
}

function scoreSunlight(answer) {
  return {
    'daily': 'strong',
    'few_days': 'okay',
    'rarely': 'weak',
    'never': 'critical',
  }[answer];
}

function scoreMovement(answer) {
  return {
    'daily': 'strong',
    '4_6_per_week': 'strong',
    '1_3_per_week': 'okay',
    'less_than_weekly': 'weak',
    'none': 'critical',
  }[answer];
}

function recommendDuration(durationOfPattern, cut): 30 | 60 | 90 {
  // Default 30
  if (!durationOfPattern || !cut) return 30;
  if (durationOfPattern === '5_plus_years') return 90;
  if (durationOfPattern === '2_5_years') return 60;
  return 30;
}
```

### 10.3 Webhook handler

```typescript
// app/api/webhooks/lemon-squeezy/route.ts

import { NextResponse } from "next/server";
import crypto from "crypto";
import { createServerClient } from "@/services/supabase/server";
import { generateProtocol } from "@/services/openai/generate-protocol";
import { sendMagicLink } from "@/services/email/send-magic-link";

export async function POST(req: Request) {
  const body = await req.text();
  const signature = req.headers.get("x-signature");

  // Verify signature
  const expectedSignature = crypto
    .createHmac("sha256", process.env.LEMON_SQUEEZY_WEBHOOK_SECRET!)
    .update(body)
    .digest("hex");

  if (signature !== expectedSignature) {
    return NextResponse.json({ error: "Invalid signature" }, { status: 401 });
  }

  const event = JSON.parse(body);
  const eventName = event.meta.event_name;
  const eventId = event.meta.event_id || event.data.id;

  const supabase = createServerClient();

  // Idempotency check
  const { data: existing } = await supabase
    .from("webhook_events")
    .select("id, processed")
    .eq("source", "lemon_squeezy")
    .eq("event_id", eventId)
    .single();

  if (existing?.processed) {
    return NextResponse.json({ ok: true, deduplicated: true });
  }

  // Insert event record
  await supabase.from("webhook_events").insert({
    source: "lemon_squeezy",
    event_id: eventId,
    event_type: eventName,
    payload: event,
    processed: false,
  });

  // Handle order_created
  if (eventName === "order_created") {
    const order = event.data;
    const customData = order.attributes.first_order_item.custom_data || {};
    const responseId = customData.response_id;
    const tier = customData.tier as 'foundation' | 'operator';
    const email = order.attributes.user_email;
    const orderId = order.id;

    if (!responseId || !tier || !email) {
      throw new Error("Missing required custom data");
    }

    // 1. Create or get user
    let userId: string;
    const { data: existingUser } = await supabase.auth.admin.listUsers();
    const found = existingUser?.users.find(u => u.email === email);

    if (found) {
      userId = found.id;
    } else {
      const { data: newUser, error } = await supabase.auth.admin.createUser({
        email,
        email_confirm: true,
      });
      if (error || !newUser.user) throw error;
      userId = newUser.user.id;
    }

    // 2. Update quiz_responses with paid status + user_id
    await supabase
      .from("quiz_responses")
      .update({
        user_id: userId,
        paid: true,
        paid_at: new Date().toISOString(),
        tier_purchased: tier,
        lemon_squeezy_order_id: orderId,
      })
      .eq("id", responseId);

    // 3. Update profile tier
    await supabase
      .from("profiles")
      .update({
        tier,
        tier_purchased_at: new Date().toISOString(),
      })
      .eq("id", userId);

    // 4. Generate protocol (this can take 10-30 seconds)
    const { data: response } = await supabase
      .from("quiz_responses")
      .select("*")
      .eq("id", responseId)
      .single();

    if (!response) throw new Error("Response not found");

    const protocolData = await generateProtocol({
      responses: response.responses,
      diagnosis: response.diagnosis_text,
      tier,
    });

    await supabase.from("protocols").insert({
      user_id: userId,
      quiz_response_id: responseId,
      tier,
      duration_days: tier === 'foundation' ? 30 : 90,
      protocol_data: protocolData,
      start_date: new Date().toISOString().split("T")[0],
    });

    // 5. Send magic link email (auto-login)
    await sendMagicLink({ email, redirectTo: "/dashboard" });

    // 6. Mark webhook as processed
    await supabase
      .from("webhook_events")
      .update({ processed: true, processed_at: new Date().toISOString() })
      .eq("source", "lemon_squeezy")
      .eq("event_id", eventId);
  }

  // Handle refunds
  if (eventName === "order_refunded") {
    const order = event.data;
    const orderId = order.id;

    // Find user, downgrade
    const { data: response } = await supabase
      .from("quiz_responses")
      .select("user_id")
      .eq("lemon_squeezy_order_id", orderId)
      .single();

    if (response?.user_id) {
      await supabase
        .from("profiles")
        .update({ tier: 'foundation', tier_purchased_at: null })
        .eq("id", response.user_id);
      // We keep the data; we just downgrade tier access.
      // RLS in app layer enforces tier checks for operator-only content.
    }

    await supabase
      .from("webhook_events")
      .update({ processed: true, processed_at: new Date().toISOString() })
      .eq("source", "lemon_squeezy")
      .eq("event_id", eventId);
  }

  return NextResponse.json({ ok: true });
}
```

## 15. Risks & mitigations

### 15.1 OpenAI prompt drift

**Risk:** Output quality varies, occasionally generic.

**Mitigation:** Save prompt versions in git. Manually QA every 50th
generation in early days. Add a "regenerate" button hidden in user
settings if first output is weak.

### 15.2 Lemon Squeezy onboarding delays

**Risk:** LS can take 3-7 days to verify Indian users.

**Mitigation:** Apply on day 1 of build. By the time you need
checkout integration (Day 8), you're verified. If LS rejects,
fallback to Polar or Paddle.

### 15.3 Apple/Google flagging "addiction" language

**Risk:** v2 mobile rejection on copy.

**Mitigation:** Already using "high-friction behavior" not "addiction"
throughout. Compliant from day one.

### 15.4 Refund disputes

**Risk:** Behavior change products have refund anxiety.

**Mitigation:** 30-day money-back guarantee, prominently displayed.
Foundation tier is $19 — low enough that few will dispute. The
diagnosis paragraph + protocol PDF are concrete deliverables they can
point to as "I got value."

### 15.5 User runs MMX as a content scrape

**Risk:** Someone takes a quiz, screenshots the diagnosis paragraph,
posts on Reddit as "I tried this app, here's what it said." Fine.
That's free marketing.

### 15.6 IG account ban (your existing risk)

**Risk:** Same as Titan — content gets reported, account banned.

**Mitigation:**
- Different IG account from Titan. Fresh aged account if possible.
- Don't DM anyone unsolicited.
- Don't reply with "DM me for the link." Always link in bio.
- No engagement bait ("comment X to get the link"). Drives reports.
- Build email list as the actual owned audience (lead magnet on
  landing page in v1.5).

### 15.7 Scope creep during build

**Risk:** You add features mid-build because "it's easy."

**Mitigation:** This document is the spec. If a feature isn't in
this document, it's not in v1. Add it to a `v2-ideas.md` file and
move on.

## 17. The non-negotiable principles (carry through every decision)

1. **Sequence matters.** Foundation before body before mind. Don't let
   users skip ahead.
2. **Lapse ≠ relapse.** Calendar UI never resets. Missed days = data,
   not failure.
3. **Identity > outcomes.** "I am becoming…" framing throughout.
4. **Autonomous motivation > controlled.** Never shame. Never threaten.
   Never use guilt mechanics.
5. **Personalization is scientific necessity.** Generic plans
   underperform; we honor that.
6. **The product itself must NOT be a supernormal stimulus.** No XP,
   no streaks-with-reset, no variable rewards, no infinite scroll. The
   cure can't be the disease.
7. **Honesty is the moat.** When the data is mixed, say so. When a
   popular claim is overstated, flag it. Don't sell certainty we don't
   have.
8. **Respect the user's attention.** Email sparingly. No marketing
   noise. No "your streak is at risk!" panic mechanics.
